Meet the Team

Instructors

Shub C. Kaur

Bhangra & Bollywood Dance Teacher

Bhangra has been part of my life since I was 8 years old. Not only do I enjoy dancing bhangra, but I am just as passionate about teaching it and taking it to another level by coaching enough bhangra dancers who can carry on this legacy by sharing it with others.

I have been teaching Bhangra since 1989 in Los Angeles, CA; Vancouver, BC; Surrey, BC; and now in Greater Seattle area and Whatcom County, WA. I currently teach Bhangra, Bollywood, Giddha and our most popular Bhangracize (aerobics with bhangra music) classes in Redmond and Lynden.

Avani Desai

Bhangra & Bollywood Dance Teacher

It was my love for dance that led me to Rhythms of India in 2010. I grew up in state of Gujarat, India and have always loved Raas/Garba/Dandia which are folk dances of the state of Gujarat.

In my passion for folk dances, I fell in love with high energy Bhangra and soon became part of the team. For the past 10 years, I have been handling all administrative work for Rhythms of India, dancing and teaching alongside Miss. Shub Kaur, and enjoying every minute of it.

Bharat Kalra

Bhangra Teacher

Growing up in the northern region of India in a punjabi family, I developed a fondness for music that eventually evolved into a passion for dancing to the rhythm. During my undergraduate studies, I was fortunate enough to become a member of a bhangra group where I began learning the traditional Punjabi folk dance.

Since then, I have never stopped dancing and have had the opportunity to perform at various events and cultural festivals, always infusing my performances with love and enthusiasm.

Teaching this beautiful art form to students of all levels is a great honor for me, as I strive to keep this rich tradition alive. I invite you to join me on this journey and experience the joy and energy that bhangra can bring. Rest assured, you will leave each class feeling energized and inspired.

Welcome to

Dance Arts School

Welcome to our vibrant dance art school, where we celebrate the infectious rhythms and electrifying moves of Bhangra and Bollywood dance forms. Our school is dedicated to promoting the rich cultural heritage and artistic expressions of India through these two dynamic dance styles.

Bhangra, a traditional folk dance from the Punjab region of India, is known for its high-energy movements, infectious beats, and vibrant costumes. With its origins deeply rooted in the agricultural traditions of the region, Bhangra represents the joy and celebration of life. At our school, we embrace the essence of Bhangra, teaching students the traditional steps and incorporating contemporary elements to create a fusion of old and new.

In addition to Bhangra, we also celebrate the glitz and glamour of Bollywood dance. Bollywood, the Hindi film industry, has captivated audiences worldwide with its colorful sets, captivating storylines, and the mesmerizing dance sequences. Bollywood dance is a fusion of various dance styles, including classical, folk, hip-hop, and modern, creating a vibrant and eclectic art form.

At our dance art school, we believe that dance is not just a physical activity but a form of self-expression and celebration of culture. Through our expert instructors, passionate about these dance forms, we offer comprehensive training programs suitable for all ages and skill levels.

Our school is more than just a place to learn dance; it is a community of like-minded individuals who share a love for Bhangra and Bollywood. We organize regular performances, workshops, and cultural events to showcase the talents of our students and spread the vibrancy of Indian dance and culture to a wider audience.

Whether you are seeking to learn Bhangra or Bollywood dance for personal enjoyment, cultural exploration, or even to pursue a professional career in dance, our dance art school is the perfect place to nurture your passion. Join us as we embark on an exhilarating journey of rhythm, expression, and celebration, immersing ourselves in the captivating world of Bhangra and Bollywood dance.
